Share2Teach is an energetic open educational resource (OER) initiative aimed at fostering a worldwide community of learners and educators. Its fundamental belief is that knowledge should be accessible, collaborative, and freely shared. The project exemplifies the strength of collective efforts, created by students under the guidance of their instructors.

The project was launched by Dr. Chantelle Bosch, a dedicated lecturer and leader in Blended Learning for Self-Directed Learning at the Research Unit of Self-Directed Learning at North-West University (NWU). Prof. Dorothy Laubscher, the UNESCO Chair on Multimodal Learning and OER, has also been instrumental in shaping the vision and direction of Share2Teach.

This platform brings together a variety of educational resources, created by students for their peers. It offers materials such as detailed semester plans and topic-specific content, all designed to boost self-directed learning through collaborative and project-based teaching methods.

Share2Teach stands as a symbol of educational innovation, inviting educators and learners around the globe to contribute, explore, and grow within this open, inclusive community. By joining, participants embark on a shared learning adventure, breaking down barriers and building connections to create a more informed and united world.
